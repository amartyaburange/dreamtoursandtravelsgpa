Mumbai Tour with Matheran

Destination Covered: Mumbai to Matheran - Mumbai


The peculiar thing about this place is its pollution free environment as no motor vehicle is permitted inside the town, making it Asia's only automobile free hill station. Fresh air, picturesque beauty and enchanting landscapes make it a serene place. Most of the tourists unwind themselves during a day trip or a weekend trip, but those who love nature won't mind spending an entire week here.

Day 01: Arrive Mumbai Airport / Railway Station

Scheduled arrive Mumbai Airport / Railway Station; meet our representative who will assist you to transfer to hotel. After fresh up explore the mesmerizing and the most populous city of India. It is also called the financial capital of India where the multinational companies flourishing in a big way. The city is established on what was once an archipelago of Seven Islands. Over the years, it saw many changes and became the very popular business hub all around the world. Start your city tour and visit Gateway of India – one of the most famous landmarks built to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to India. Prince of Wales Museum – close of the Gateway of India has a priceless collection of Art, sculpture, painting & traditional utensils followed by Siddhi Vinayak Temple, Dhobi Ghat, Haji Ali Mosque and Choupatty Beach. Evening free for Shopping. Overnight stay at Hotel.

Day 02: Mumbai - Excursion of Matheran Tour ( 60 Kms, one way )

Take breakfast in the hotel and later get ready for same day excursion of Matheran is a Hill Station and a municipal council in the Raigad district in the Indian state of Maharashtra. It is a hill station in Karjat Tahsil and is also the smallest hill station in India. It is located on the Western Ghats range at an elevation of around 800 m ( 2,625 feet ) above sea level. It is located around 90 km from Mumbai, and 120 km from Pune. Matheran's proximity to many metropolitan cities makes it a popular weekend getaway for urban residents. Matheran, which means "forest on the forehead" ( of the mountains ), is an eco-sensitive region, declared by the Ministry of Environment and Forest, Government of India. It is Asia's only automobile-free hill station. Rest of time free for your own activities. Overnight stay at Hotel.

Day 03: Mumbai – Excursion of Elephant Caves ( By Ferry Boat )

Take breakfast in the hotel and later scheduled transfer to Gateway of India Jetty to take the morning boat for the excursion of Elephant Caves. The island, located on an arm of the Arabian Sea, consists of two groups of caves—the first is a large group of five Hindu caves, the second, a smaller group of two Buddhist caves. The Hindu caves contain rock cut stone sculptures, representing the Shaiva Hindu set, dedicated to the god Shiva. The rock cut architecture of the caves has been dated to between the 5th and 8th centuries, although the identity of the original builders is still a subject of debate. The caves are hewn from solid basalt rock. All the caves were also originally painted in the past, but now only traces remain. Back to hotel by 1400Hrs. Rest of time free for your own activities / shopping. Later in the evening scheduled transfer to Airport / Railway station to board the flight / train for onward journey. Tour ends with happy memories.
